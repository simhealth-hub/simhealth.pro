1. General

Plain-language overview of what SimHealth is and what it is not.

1a. What is SimHealth?

SimHealth is a clinician-informed digital project in development exploring how structured, safety-first tools may support people experiencing gambling-related urges. It is designed to complement existing care pathways and is currently in a pre-pilot phase.

1b. Who is SimHealth for?

SimHealth is intended for adults seeking structured, reflective support related to gambling urges, as well as clinicians and researchers interested in supervised evaluation and governance-minded digital support tools.

1c. What stage is the project at?

SimHealth is in a pre-pilot / pilot-planning stage. Content and features may change based on testing, feedback, and safety review. Public communication is intentionally conservative until appropriate evaluation is completed.

1d. What does “clinician-informed” mean?

“Clinician-informed” means healthcare professionals have contributed advisory input during design. It does not imply clinical validation, endorsement, or regulatory approval.

2. Medical, safety & boundaries

Clear boundaries to prevent confusion and protect patient safety.

2a. Is SimHealth a medical device?

No. SimHealth is not a medical device and is not currently regulated as one. It does not diagnose, treat, prevent, or cure medical conditions.

2b. Does SimHealth provide medical advice or treatment?

No. SimHealth does not provide medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. It is not a substitute for professional healthcare or clinical judgment.

2c. Is SimHealth for emergencies or crises?

No. SimHealth is not an emergency or crisis service, and the website is not monitored for urgent situations. If there is immediate risk, use local emergency services or crisis support organisations.

2d. What should I do if I feel at risk?

If you feel at immediate risk of harm, contact your local emergency number now. If you need urgent emotional support, use official crisis services listed on our Crisis Resources page.

3. Research & pilots

How evaluation is planned (pilot-first), and how clinicians can engage.

3a. Is SimHealth clinically validated?

Not yet. SimHealth has not undergone clinical validation. Any future outcomes will only be communicated publicly when supported by appropriate evaluation and governance.

3b. How will evaluation be done?

The project is designed for staged evaluation — beginning with small supervised pilots focused on feasibility, safety, and usability. Progression would depend on governance readiness and clinical oversight.

You can read the current public overview on the Research page.

3c. Can clinicians or institutions participate in a pilot?

Potentially. Pilot access (when available) is intended to be supervised and governance-led. If you represent a service or institution, the best route is the pilot enquiry page.

4. Privacy, data & security

Transparent, GDPR-aligned expectations for early-stage development.

4a. Does SimHealth collect personal or health data?

At this stage, SimHealth collects minimal information (for example, an email address if you subscribe to updates). Any future pilot data collection would be optional, consent-based, and limited to what is necessary for evaluation.

4b. How is data protected?

Data protection is taken seriously. Where applicable, data handling is designed to align with GDPR principles including minimisation, purpose limitation, and user rights. Access is restricted and data use is governed.

For full details, see Privacy.

4c. Can I delete my data or unsubscribe?

Yes. You can unsubscribe from updates at any time. You can also request deletion of your data by contacting contact@simhealth.pro.

4d. Does SimHealth use AI?

SimHealth may explore AI-assisted features in future, but any such work will be governed conservatively and evaluated appropriately. SimHealth does not provide automated medical decisions, diagnosis, or treatment recommendations.
