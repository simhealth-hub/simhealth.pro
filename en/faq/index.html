About SimHealth

What is SimHealth?

SimHealth is a clinician-informed digital project in development, exploring structured support for people experiencing gambling-related urges — especially during high-risk moments when support may be unavailable.

Who is SimHealth for?

SimHealth is being designed for:

  • Patients: who want structured, non-judgemental support during urges.
  • Clinicians and services: who may want supervised pilot evaluation tools.
  • Researchers: interested in feasibility, safety, and real-world evaluation.

What problem is it trying to address?

Gambling-related harm often occurs outside clinical settings — during late-night urges, stress spikes, or impulse windows. SimHealth explores whether carefully governed digital support can help people pause, reflect, and choose safer actions in those moments.

What is SimHealth not?

  • It is not emergency or crisis care.
  • It is not a substitute for professional treatment.
  • It does not diagnose conditions or provide medical advice.
  • It does not claim proven outcomes without appropriate evaluation.

Safety and boundaries

Is SimHealth emergency support or crisis care?

No. SimHealth is not monitored as an emergency service and does not provide crisis intervention. If you are in immediate danger, contact your local emergency services.

What should I do if I feel unsafe or at risk?

Use official real-world help immediately. On this site, go to Crisis Resources for country-specific services.

Does SimHealth give medical advice?

No. SimHealth content is informational and supportive in nature and is not a replacement for clinical judgment. Always seek help from qualified professionals for diagnosis or treatment decisions.

Can SimHealth diagnose or treat a condition?

No. SimHealth does not diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease or condition. Any future evaluation would be communicated carefully and only after appropriate oversight.

Clinical and research positioning

Is SimHealth a medical device?

SimHealth is presented publicly as a project in development, with conservative claims and pilot-first intentions. Regulatory classification depends on the final intended use and functionality. Public wording is designed to avoid over-claiming and to keep boundaries explicit.

What does “clinician-informed” mean?

It means the project direction and safety boundaries are being shaped with clinical input. This does not automatically imply clinical validation or endorsement, and we avoid implying that it does.

What stage is the project at?

Pre-pilot / pilot planning. The focus is on safety-first design, governance readiness, and staged evaluation.

How will outcomes be shared?

We do not publish outcome claims without appropriate evaluation. If pilot work progresses, public updates will focus on feasibility and safety, and only discuss outcomes once they are properly supported.

Privacy, data, and consent

Do you collect personal data?

If you submit a form (for example, project updates), we may collect the information you provide (like your email). We aim to minimise collection and use it only for the stated purpose.

Do you collect health data?

Public pages are designed to avoid collecting sensitive health information by default. Any future pilot-related data design would be consent-led and described clearly to participants.

How is data protected?

We use standard web protections (HTTPS) and design toward access restriction and minimised collection. Details are documented in the relevant privacy policy pages.

Can I ask you to delete my data?

Yes. You can request deletion by contacting contact@simhealth.pro. Your rights and related details are described in the privacy policy.

Using the website

Do you use cookies or analytics?

If cookies or analytics are used, they should be described in the cookie policy and privacy policy. SimHealth aims to keep tracking conservative and transparent.

Is the site accessible?

Accessibility is a priority and the project aims to support clear navigation, readable typography, keyboard use, and accessibility-aware design patterns. See the Accessibility page for the current statement.

How can I contact you?

Use the Contact page for professional enquiries. If you need urgent support, use Crisis Resources.
