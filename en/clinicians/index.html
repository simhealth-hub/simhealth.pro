For Clinicians

A clinically informed digital companion designed to support patients between sessions and during high-risk moments.

Clinical intent

SimHealth is being developed as a supportive digital companion for individuals working to manage gambling-related urges. The aim is to provide structured support during moments of elevated risk, while reinforcing safer decision-making and continuity of care.

SimHealth is intended to complement established approaches (e.g., CBT-informed strategies and exposure-based models) and is not positioned as a standalone treatment.

How SimHealth may support care

  • Provide structured support between appointments, when urges often intensify
  • Encourage a pause-and-reflect response during high-risk moments
  • Support patient engagement and reduce drop-off between sessions
  • Offer a controlled environment designed around safety and responsibility
  • Enable future pilot evaluation within ethically governed clinical settings

Safety and boundaries

SimHealth is not a gambling product and does not involve real money. It is being developed with an emphasis on risk reduction, patient safety, and responsible use. It is not intended for crisis situations and does not replace professional judgement or clinical care.

Current status

SimHealth is currently in development. Initial access will be limited to pilot collaborations and small-scale clinical evaluation. Clinician-facing resources and guidance will be provided as the programme progresses.
