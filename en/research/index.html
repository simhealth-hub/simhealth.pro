Research

Building clinically informed digital support for gambling-related urges — designed for careful evaluation, ethical governance, and patient safety from day one.

Evidence

SimHealth is being developed as a structured digital companion to support people experiencing gambling-related urges. The direction is informed by established behavioural health principles commonly used in clinical practice, including skills-based strategies and exposure-informed approaches.

The intent is not to “prove an app works overnight”, but to evaluate whether supervised, well-governed digital support can: improve continuity of care, reduce disengagement between sessions, and support safer decision-making during high-risk moments.

Clinical alignment: SimHealth is designed to complement clinical care, not replace it. Where crisis risk is present, the correct response is real-world emergency and crisis services.

Methodology

SimHealth is designed for staged evaluation — beginning with small supervised pilots and progressing only when safety, governance, and clinical oversight are in place. This programme is built for careful iteration, not uncontrolled release.

Staged evaluation approach

  • Phase 0 — Feasibility design: safety-first UX, crisis signposting, consent flows, governance documentation.
  • Phase 1 — Supervised pilot: small cohort, clinician oversight, feasibility + safety signals.
  • Phase 2 — Expanded evaluation (if justified): broader study design and formal oversight as required.

Data handling (privacy-first)

Where data collection is included in a pilot, it is intended to be strictly optional and used only with explicit participant consent. Collection should be limited to what is necessary for evaluation and improvement and structured to align with EU GDPR principles.

  • Consent-led: opt-in participation, clear explanations, and withdrawal where applicable.
  • Data minimisation: collect the minimum necessary; avoid unnecessary identifiers.
  • Protection: anonymise where feasible; otherwise pseudonymise + restrict access.
  • Purpose limitation: no secondary use without appropriate governance.

Outcomes

Outcomes will only be discussed publicly when supported by appropriate evaluation. Early-stage work focuses on feasibility, safety, and engagement — not medical claims.

  • Feasibility: safe and consistent use in real pathways
  • Engagement: continuity between sessions
  • Safety signals: reduced harm risk
  • Clinical usability: minimal burden
  • Research readiness: structure for larger studies if justified
Positioning note: SimHealth is in development and is not emergency care. Any future research programme should be conducted with appropriate governance and professional oversight.
