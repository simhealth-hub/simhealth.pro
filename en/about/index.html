About SimHealth

SimHealth is a clinician-informed digital project exploring support for gambling-related urges — designed for safety, governance, and staged evaluation. It is not emergency care.

Stage: Pre-pilot / evaluation design No medical claims • Not a medical device • Not monitored as crisis support
Get project updates Request pilot info

Mission

SimHealth is being developed to support people who experience intense gambling urges — especially in short, high-risk windows where relapse often happens. The goal is grounded, responsible digital support that can fit within real care pathways, without making medical claims.

What SimHealth aims to do

  • Reduce harm: encourage safer choices during craving spikes and emotionally charged moments.
  • Support continuity: bridge gaps between sessions and reduce drop-off.
  • Respect clinical reality: complement care — not replace clinicians, services, or crisis support.
  • Keep trust: privacy-first design, clear boundaries, transparent language.
Important: SimHealth is in development and is not presented as a medical device or emergency service. If you need urgent help, use Crisis Resources.

Principles

The project is designed to be evaluated responsibly, with clear boundaries and governance. Public language is intentionally conservative: no outcomes are presented without appropriate evaluation.

Frameworks (public summary)

  • CET+: a working label for a cue-exposure-informed approach prioritising safety, reflection, and structured coping.
  • ACUI: Adaptive Clinical User Interface — accessibility- and cognition-aware UX (readable contrast, reduced overload, clear pathways).
Positioning: SimHealth is intended to complement clinical care, not replace it. Where crisis risk is present, the correct response is real-world emergency and crisis services.

Governance & boundaries

  • No emergency claims. Clear signposting to real services.
  • No “magic cure” language. Evaluation before public outcomes.
  • Consent-led. Any data use is opt-in and minimised.
  • Clinical oversight. Pilot use only when appropriate.
Privacy: Any data collection in pilots is intended to follow GDPR principles. See Privacy for details.
