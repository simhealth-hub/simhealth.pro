About SimHealth

A safety-first, clinician-informed project exploring digital support for gambling-related urges — designed to reduce harm, not enable it.

Get project updates Request pilot info

Mission

SimHealth is being developed to support people who experience intense gambling urges — especially in short, high-risk windows where relapse often happens. The goal is to deliver grounded, responsible digital support that fits into real care pathways, without making medical claims.

What SimHealth aims to do

  • Reduce harm: encourage safer choices during craving spikes and emotionally charged moments.
  • Support continuity: bridge gaps between sessions and reduce drop-off.
  • Respect clinical reality: complement care — not replace clinicians, services, or crisis support.
  • Keep trust: privacy-first design, clear boundaries, and transparent language.
Important: SimHealth is in development and is not presented as a medical device or emergency service. If you need urgent help, use Crisis Resources.

Model overview

SimHealth sits at the intersection of three priorities: evidence-informed behavioural principles, adaptive user experience, and safe clinical governance. The goal is a digital tool that can be evaluated responsibly and used only in appropriate contexts.

Concept map

This is a high-level public view. Protocol details, measures, and clinical workflows are documented and shared only in appropriate contexts.

What CET+ means (public summary)

CET+ is a working label for a cue-exposure-informed approach that prioritises safety, reflection, and structured coping, while being compatible with supervised pilots and iterative evaluation.

What ACUI means (public summary)

ACUI (Adaptive Clinical User Interface) is the idea that the experience should adjust for accessibility and cognitive needs (e.g., ADHD-friendly layouts, readable contrast, reduced overload) — without sacrificing clarity or clinical seriousness.

Governance & boundaries

  • No emergency claims. Clear signposting to real services.
  • No “magic cure” language. Evaluation before outcomes are discussed.
  • Consent-led. Any data use is opt-in and minimised.
  • Clinical oversight. Pilot use only when appropriate.
Privacy: Any data collection in pilots is intended to follow GDPR principles. See Privacy for details.
