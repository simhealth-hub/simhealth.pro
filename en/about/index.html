About SimHealth

A clinical digital companion in development, designed to support people managing gambling-related urges with safety and dignity.

SimHealth is being developed with a clear principle: when the stakes are real, the design must be responsible. This project prioritises patient safety, ethical boundaries, and careful clinical collaboration over speed.

Mission

SimHealth exists to support people who experience gambling-related urges, especially during short windows when risk is elevated. The intention is to offer structured support that helps create space between the urge and the action, reinforcing safer decisions.

Reduce harm Support safer choices when urges are strongest, without judgement.
Strengthen continuity Encourage engagement between sessions and recovery milestones.
Complement care Designed to support existing treatment approaches, not replace them.
Build trust Developed with a safety-first mindset and clear boundaries.

Ethics

SimHealth is being developed for a sensitive clinical context. That means design decisions must prioritise safety, privacy, and responsible use — even when that makes development slower.

Not a gambling product No real-money gambling, and no incentive to chase losses.
Safety over engagement We avoid manipulative design patterns that amplify compulsive behaviour.
Privacy-first Any future data use would be consent-led, minimised, and governed appropriately.
Clear boundaries SimHealth is not a replacement for professional care and not for crisis situations.

Important: SimHealth is in development. It is presented as a support tool and is not making medical claims. Access will be introduced cautiously through pilot collaborations when appropriate governance is in place.

Development approach

SimHealth is being built using a pilot-led approach: develop, review, test in controlled settings, then iterate. This process is designed to protect patients, support clinicians, and ensure the project remains ethically aligned.

Stage-gated release Progress only when safety, usability, and governance requirements are met.
Clinical collaboration Shaped through feedback loops with healthcare professionals and partners.
Accessibility and inclusion Designed for clarity, reduced friction, and inclusive usability.
Evaluation mindset Outcomes will be discussed publicly only when appropriately supported.
