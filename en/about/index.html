About SimHealth

A clinician-informed digital project exploring how structured, safety-first tools may support people experiencing gambling-related urges — designed with explicit safety and governance boundaries.

Stage: Pre-pilot No medical claims • Not a medical device • Not crisis care
Request pilot information Get updates

Mission

SimHealth focuses on high-risk moments where gambling urges escalate rapidly and traditional support is often unavailable. The project explores whether carefully governed digital tools can support safer decision-making within real clinical pathways.

Gambling-related harm often occurs outside clinical settings, during moments when support is unavailable.

What SimHealth is designed to do

  • Reduce harm: support safer choices during acute craving windows.
  • Support continuity: reduce disengagement between clinical sessions.
  • Complement care: function alongside clinicians, not replace them.
  • Maintain trust: privacy-first design and conservative public claims.
Important: SimHealth does not provide emergency support. If you are at risk, use Crisis Resources .

Who this project is for

  • Patients: seeking structured, non-judgemental support.
  • Clinicians: exploring supervised digital adjuncts.
  • Researchers: interested in feasibility and safety evaluation.

Design principles

SimHealth is built around explicit boundaries. Public communication is intentionally conservative until appropriate evaluation is completed.

Frameworks (public overview)

  • CET+: cue-exposure-informed, reflection-first approach.
  • ACUI: Adaptive Clinical User Interface — accessibility-aware UX (User Experience).
What “UX” means: UX = “User Experience” — how easy, clear, and supportive a product feels to use (especially under stress).
Positioning: Any future use is intended to occur under professional oversight and appropriate governance.

Governance boundaries

  • No emergency claims
  • No outcome statements without evaluation
  • Consent-led data use only
  • Clinical oversight required for pilots
Privacy: All pilot data design aligns with GDPR principles. See Privacy.
